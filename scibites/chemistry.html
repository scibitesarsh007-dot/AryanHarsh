Chem Bite #1

Balancing Equations: The Sandwich Rule 🥪

Have you ever seen a crazy chemical equation like H₂ + O₂ → H₂O and thought, "Wait... where did the second oxygen atom go?"

You're absolutely right to be suspicious. Atoms aren't magicians; they can't just pull a disappearing act!

The Rule of The Universe (Law of Conservation of Mass): You can't create stuff out of nowhere, and you can't destroy it. Whatever goes into the recipe MUST come out.

📝 Chemistry Class 10: Chemical Equations Notes

1. The Core Principle
The Law of Conservation of Mass dictates that mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. Therefore, the total mass of reactantsMUST equal the total mass of products.

2. Unbalanced Equation (Skeletal Equation)
Example: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
This equation is physically impossible on its own because we lose one Oxygen atom on the right side.

3. Step-by-Step Balancing (The "Sandwich" Method)
- Step A: Check the ingredients (atoms) left vs right.
- Step B: Find the unbalanced atom. Here, Oxygen has 2 on the left, but only 1 on the right.
- Step C: Add a multiplier strictly to the FRONT of the molecules. (We put a '2' in front of H₂O). Now we have 2 Oxygen on the right. Perfect.
- Step D: Re-check. Uh oh, now we have 4 Hydrogen on the right (2 x 2), but only 2 on the left!
- Step E: Put a '2' in front of the Hydrogen on the left side to compensate. (2 H₂).

4. Final Balanced Equation
2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O (Everything is equal now!)
